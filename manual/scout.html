Scout

pros: longest LOS-radius
cons: a very weak class in combat

Abilities

  • Infliltration (disguise yourself as the enemy)
  • No terrain penalty
  • Quiet swimmer
  • Trap detection
  • Spot enemies for teammates

The main special ability of scouts is infiltration, also known as disguising.

When you see a corpse of an enemy (a '%' of the opposing team's colour), walk over it and hit space to steal the clothes of the corpse. This takes a few turns, after which you will appear to your enemies in their colour. Your own teammates will see you as a brown '@'.

Your disguise falls off if you attack an enemy. The enemy can also notice that you are not who you seem to be by other means and 'test attack' you: if they walk into you, they'll carry out a melee attack, whereas if you really were a teammate that wouldn't happen. (An explanation for the realists: you fail in the secret hand-shake.) Also, you won't steal the 'identity' of the corpse: if an enemy turns on her titles, she will notice that you are a 'teammate without a name', an infiltrator.

Scouts are also skilled in detecting Traps, do not suffer a time penalty for walking over rought terrain or swamp, and don't make noise when swimming.

Finally, if a scout sees an enemy PC, this PC is automatically shown to all nearby teammates, even if outside of their LOS. For instance: 

             .........
enemy PC --> .@.....@. <-- a scout
             ######...
                  #...
                  #@.. <-- the scout's teammate
Here, the enemy PC will be drawn for the scout's teammate, although outside of her LOS.
