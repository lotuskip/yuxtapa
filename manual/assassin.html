Assassin

pros: very high DV, very accurate melee attack
cons: no PV, lowish HPs, no torch

Abilities

  • Hiding (in the dark, by walls)
  • Backstabbing
  • Quiet swimmer
  • Flash bombs
  • Immune to blinding

Hiding ability: whenever you walk into a non-lit tile (see Vision and hearing) that is next to a wall tile and no enemy within a 3 tiles distance sees you there, you become invisible to all enemies.

You become visible again if you either move so that one of the above conditions is not satisfied, or if an enemy walks 'right at you', which technically means: is facing at a square that is at most 1 square away from the square you're at.

To reiterate: as long as you stay still, the area becoming lit or even the neighbouring wall getting dug away by a friendly miner does not make you visible: only your moving or an enemy walking into you makes you visible again.

Note that if you are carrying the treasure item (see Game types), any movement leaves a visible trail that remains for a number of turns, thus making it extremely difficult to remain hidden. (The enemies might not see you, i.e. your '@', but they very likely see the background colour that indicates the treasure item's movement.)

If you attack an enemy in melee while hiding, your attack is more deadly (see Rule mechanics for details). This is sometimes referred to as backstabbing.

The other ability of Assassins is to activate 'light bombs'. These act as if a light trap (see Traps) was activated at the assassin's location. Assassins themselves are immune to blindness — both from their own light bombs and from light traps.

You only have 3 light bombs per spawning at your disposal.

Finally, assassins do not make noise when they swim in water.
