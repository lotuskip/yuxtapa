Objects in the game

Boulders

O
Boulders are inanimate objects that some PCs can push around. To push a boulder, simply walk against it. Boulders can only be pushed on level ground, not on rough terrain or in swamp. Classes that can push boulders are Fighter, Mindcrafter, Miner, and Trapper. Pushing a boulder takes twice as long as regular movement.

Miners can also destroy boulders, by mining them. This requires that the boulder is 'blocked'. Destroying all the boulders in a certain area can be the mission objective; see Game types. In a dungeon (see Map types), a boulder can also be destroyed by pushing it into a chasm.

Arrows or spells will not pass boulders, and you cannot walk over a boulder that you cannot push. Boulders, however, do not block sight or light.

Boulders cannot be pushed through doorways or on top of flags. Pushing a boulder into water destroys the boulder and creates a bridge (appears as a floor tile). Boulders can be used to block portal exits (see below), preventing the usage of the portal (and effectively also hiding the portal from sight).

Boulder sources

_
If a Miner mines a boulder source, a new boulder is created. For other classes a boulder source has no actual effect. However, if a PC is standing on the boulder source when a miner finishes carving a boulder, that PC is killed and the miner gets the kill.

Corpses

%, %
These are often left when PCs die (see ways to die). They will vanish quite quickly, so seeing them is usually a sign of a recent battle. They are of particular importance to scouts.

Doors

\ (open)
+ (closed)
Doors are basically 'walls that you can open and close'. When closed, they are inpassable and cannot be seen through. When open, they are just like any ground or floor tile, except that boulders cannot pass them.

A closed door is opened simply by walking against it.

To close a door, use the command 'c' (default). This will close any door next to you. If there are several doors next to you, the one closest to your facing direction is picked. If two doors are equally possible, you will close either one (not at random, but according to certain logic not worth being explained here).

Flags

&, &, &
Flags are where player characters spawn. See Limbo and Spawning and Game types.

Portals

0 (two-way)
A (one-way entry)
V (one-way exit)
When you walk into a portal other than 1-way exit, you are instantly teleported to another portal. You must actually walk in; blinking onto a portal or moving there by switcing position won't teleport you. You also won't be teleported if there is no exit available (an exit is unavailable if someone is standing on it or has pushed a boulder to block it).

Two-way portals are linked in a chain. When you walk into one, you are teleported to the next one in the chain, or if that one is blocked, the next one after that etc. When walking into a one-way portal entry, you are teleported to any random 1-way exit, if there are unblocked ones left.

Torches

t
These light a certain radius (see Vision and hearing). They can be used to light a portable torch carried by a PC (stand on the 't' and hit the appropriate key, 'u' by default).
