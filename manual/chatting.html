Chatting and shouting

The commands c and s (default) put you in typing mode where you can type something to say; hit enter to say it, or tab/esc to cancel (hitting enter when you haven't typed anything also cancels, so you won't 'say nothing'.)

The difference between these commands is that shouting is an 'in-game' feature while chatting is considered 'off-game'. Every connected player sees what you say in the chat. But when you shout, it's not you who shouts but your character! Only the players whose characters are close enough to your character will see what you say. This includes spectators. Shouting also triggers a sound event, see Vision and hearing. If a PC shouts in your line of sight, her '@' is replaced by a '!' for one turn.

On occasion, shouts are generated automatically. At the moment this happens if someone falls into a chasm.

Chat commands

The chat can also be used to send certain commands and requests to the server. These always start with an exclamation mark.

Usable by anyone, anytime:

  • !serverinfo prints information about the server's settings into the chat
  • !adminlvl [nick] prints the admin level of the player with the specified nick
  • !teams prints the current teams
  • !dontstatme: tell the server not to save your statistics; works per-session only (this is not intended so much for shy human players to keep their performance secret, but is used by the Bots)

Requiring AL at least 2:

  • !shuffle shuffles the teams, see Team balance
  • !nextmap forces an Intermission, or, if on one, forces it to end

Requiring AL higher than the target's:

  • !kick [nick] kicks a player off of the server
  • !mute [nick] prevents the chat messages of a player from showing (not just to the issuer but to anyone)
  • !unmute [nick] remove a mute
  • !setal [nick] [value] sets a player's admin level; see Admin levels

Requiring AL at least 3 and higher than the target's:

  • !ban [nick] kicks a player and bans their IP
  • !clearbans removes all bans (otherwise they remain until server restart)
  • !teambal [o/a/p] sets the Team balance option ([o]ff, [a]ctive, [p]assive)
  • !servermsg [new serverinfo] sets the serverinfo
  • !spawnbots [num] spawn num-many Bots; must be a single digit, so at most 9
  • !dropbots remove all bots (individual bots can of course be '!kick'ed, too)
The last two commands only affect a session; they do not overwrite the settings in the Server configuration.

In all the commands that contain [nick], you can use a partial nick. For instance, if there is an 'anonymous' playing and you issue '!kick ano', and there is no other player with 'ano' in their name, 'anonymous' gets kicked.

Note that banning really bans the entire IP. This can have an undesired side-effect of banning a whole subnet of people when one player from that IP gets a ban. Also, if you ban bots, that bans the local IP making further spawning of bots impossible.
