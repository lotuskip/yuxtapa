Mindcrafter

pros: pretty good in all respects except:
cons: lowish HPs, no torch

Abilities

  • Blink (random teleport)
  • can push boulders

The Mindcrafters can blink themselves away (or into) danger by simply hitting space. This will effectively teleport the mindcrafter instantly to a random square which is:

  • not occupied
  • passable
  • within the mindcrafter's line of sight, or just outside of it
The target position will be the furthest one possible, or a random one of those if there are several options.

Some points to consider, though:

The blinking really is instantaneous; consider the following example. 

@ <-- the Mc

    @ <-- enemy PC
   X <-- point where the Mc blinks
Here we assume that the enemy PC is moving SW on the same turn, but that the blinking action takes place before the move. The result: the enemy PC gets to attack the Mc for free. On the other hand, if the enemy's move took place first, the blinking destination could not be at 'X'; the enemy PC would now be there!

Another thing to keep in mind is that the destination square only needs to be vacant. This means that you can blink into water and drown or into a chasm and die instantly. If there is water or chasm in your LOS, only use blinking if the risk is worth taking! (If you want to, for instance, cross a chasm by blinking, you usually should blink right at the edge of the chasm to minimise the risk.)

It can happen that there are no free squares to blink to. Then you simply won't blink after all. This counts as an action, though, not as a turn spent waiting.

The blinking ability is limited as described here.
