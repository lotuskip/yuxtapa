Map types

There are two main types of maps in yuxtapa: dungeon and outdoor. Both of these can be either inhabited, which means that there are a large number of houses and static torches (see Objects in the Game.

Both map types have level ground, rough ground, rock, water, and houses. Dungeons have also chasms, while outdoor maps have roads&grass (just like level ground, only different colour), trees, and swamp. Outdoor houses can have windows (you can see but not pass through them).

Dungeon type maps consists of caverns, corridors, rooms, and 'nests' (very crude and concealed caverns). They can contain ''chasms'', falling to which will kill a player character. At least at the moment, dungeons do not contain water or trees.

Trappers might be said to have a disadvantage in dungeons due to the fact that there are no trees. Miners are of particular importance in dungeons, since without them movement can be very limited.

In outdoor maps trappers are particularly powerful. Firstly, because there are lots of trees giving them opportunities to hide, and secondly, because they can move in swamp unhindered unlike most other classes. On the other hand, the openness of the outdoor maps makes using traps less effective. Assassins are often weaker outdoors, due to the small amount of walls.

Wizards can be invaluable in uninhabited maps, because they can light their torches without an existing static torch (and teammates can then light their torchers using the wizard's torch).
