Admin levels

Every player on a server has an admin level (AL) on a server. The ALs are

  • 0, guest, (G)
  • 1, regular, (R)
  • 2, trusted user, (TU)
  • 3, admin, (A)
  • 4, super admin, (SA)

Every newly connected player is initially a guest. There can be only one super admin per server.

To check the AL of yourself or anyone else, you can use the !adminlvl command, see Chatting and shouting. All the ALs of all connected players are also automatically displayed during intermission.

The Super Admin

To become a super admin, you must be the first player to connect to the server, and execute the command 

!setal [your-nick-here] 4
This command (see Chatting and shouting) sets the AL of a user. Usually you can only change the AL of other users, but in the special case that you are the first and only player on the server, you can make yourself a super admin.

Privileges

Your AL determines what you are entitled to do on the server, executing commands wise.

You may only set the AL (!setal) of a player whose AL currently is lower than yours, and you can set their AL to atmost your own AL. There can be only one super admin, though.

You may only kick, ban, mute, or unmute (!kick, !ban, !mute, !unmute) players with lower AL than your own. Moreover, to ban you must be an admin (AL at least 3).

The commands !shuffle, !nextmap, and !teambalance are usable by trusted users and admins (AL at least 2).
