Admin levels

Every player on a server has an admin level (AL). The possible ALs are

  • 0, guest, (G)
  • 1, regular, (R)
  • 2, trusted user, (TU)
  • 3, admin, (A)
  • 4, super admin, (SA)

Every new player is initially a guest. There can be only one super admin on a server.

To check the AL of yourself or anyone else, you can use the !adminlvl command, see Chatting and shouting. All the ALs of all connected players are also automatically displayed during intermission.

The Super Admin

To become the super admin, you must be the first player to connect to the server, and execute the command 

!setal [your-nick-here] 4
This command (see Chatting and shouting) sets the AL of a user. Usually you can only change the AL of other users, but in the special case that you are the first and only player on the server, you can make yourself the super admin.

Privileges

Your AL determines what you are entitled to do on the server, or, what commands you are allowed to execute. See Chatting and shouting for the list of the commands and their requirements.

You may only set the AL (!setal) of a player whose AL is currently lower than yours, and you can set their AL at most to equal your own AL. There can be only one super admin, though.
