Strategy guide

To be a really good player, you really need to figure these things out for yourself through experience and go beyond these rules of thumb. Be cunning.

Playing with each class

Archer: Try to stay close to a melee-capable teammate who can protect you while you shoot. If such is not available, flee from melee. It's often best not to use your torch, but to shoot from the dark into the light. Try to get to positions that are easily defended (protected by chasm/water/rough terrain) or allow for a sure-hit line of fire (end of a narrow corridor). Trees are a nuisance only if they are further away from you; prefer to shoot from among trees as opposed to into trees.

Assassin: Consider collaborating with scouts: they reveal the enemy so you know when it is safe to move without being spotted. Try to take advantage of light traps.

Combat mage: Zaps are effective in narrow spaces and in close range (often it's better to zap than to melee). When sure that teammates are behind your back, feel free to zap blindly at where you think the enemy might be. Remember that zaps travel both faster and further when they are bouncing off of walls. Beware of archers who easily ward off your zaps, wizards who have a good chance of resisting them, and other combat magi who have both of these traits.

Fighter: You can succesfully melee anyone. Incomplete circle strikes are often better than normal melee, except against high-DV opponents (assassins and archers). Be extra careful with spellcasters: wizards in open spaces and combat magi in close range.

Healer: Poison and run — you can't win in melee except by getting lucky. If you're hit and survive, heal up as soon as possible. Help out teammates instead of soloing.

Mindcrafer: A hard-to-define class. The special ability is certainly not the "point" of this class. Good for scouting and melee, particularly against spellcasters. Keep in mind that you can blink to anywhere you can see, in particular, through otherwise unpassable windows.

Miner: Strong in melee and weak against spellcasters just like fighters. The special ability is absolutely crucial and should be used a lot. If there is no need to mine passages or to build bridges, there is no point playing a miner.

Scout: Always do scouting, don't bother with anything else. Scouting specifically means pointing out enemies and detecting traps. Collaborate with archers and assassins. Don't drop a disguise without a good reason.

Trapper: Trap all critical pathways, but in case the area is not lit, leave a safe passage for teammates to walk without risk. Also detect and disarm enemy traps. Even though not terrible in melee, using a trapper for that would be a waste. With their hiding in trees ability they make good scout replacements in outdoor maps.

Wizard: In narrow and curvy passages don't even bother doing anything besides offering a light. Stay in open spaces, passages surrounded by chasm or water are the best. Outdoors, avoid forests, as the trees can unexpectedly absorb your missiles.

Planewalker: Use for quickly scouting the territory, particularly useful for spotting objective-related objects like boulders in destroy mode and the item in steal mode. Communicate with miners. Also spy what the enemy is up to and tell your teammates. There is often no reason to move from the initial spawn flag.

Dealing with each class

Archer: spells or backstab are the best, melee works too if your own class has good tohit. Use corners and boulders for cover, or trees if nothing else is available.

Assassin: magic missiles are great (they both spot and often kill assassins for you).

Combat mage: backstab or arrows, or outnumber them in melee.

Fighter: spells.

Healer: anything besides magic missiles and arrows, since they usually can survive these and heal up. Make sure you have a friendly healer around to heal you in case you're poisoned.

Wizard: if the magic missiles give you trouble, just run around in zig-zag making them hit each other, or flee into some trees.

Advanced team cooperation

On a large map with a conveniently located boulder source, a team (typically the purple team in conquest or steal mode) can organise to build fortifications out of boulders. A line of two boulders is inpassable without miners or mindcrafters. Combine with the use of boobytraps.
